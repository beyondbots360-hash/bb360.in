## The Hidden Cost of Website Builders

Many freelancers start by looking at website builders like Wix, Squarespace, or Webflow. They advertise ease of use and low starting costs. But the reality is quite different once you calculate the actual expenses.

### Recurring Subscriptions Add Up
To use your own domain and remove branding, you will pay between $16 and $29 per month. Over three years, that is $576 to $1,044 just to keep your portfolio online. That does not include the cost of your domain name or professional email.

### Speed and Performance Issues
Templates are bloated. They load unnecessary scripts and heavy stylesheets that slow down your page. If a potential client opens your portfolio on their phone and it takes more than three seconds to load, they will close the tab. This speed lag directly hurts your search rankings on Google.

### The Learning Curve Time Drain
"No-code" does not mean no effort. You will spend dozens of hours tweaking alignments, resizing images, and trying to get forms to work. Your time is worth money. If you charge $50 an hour, spending 20 hours building your own site costs you $1,000 in lost billable time.

## Why a Single-Page Portfolio Converts Better

A multi-page website often distracts visitors. When a prospective client lands on your site, they want to know three things:
1. Who you are and what you do.
2. What your work looks like.
3. How to hire you.

By putting all this information on a single, well-structured page, you guide the reader through a logical sales funnel. There are no extra links to click, no slow page transitions, and no navigation menus to get lost in.

```
[ Hero Header & Core Value Proposition ]
                  │
                  ▼
[ Selected Work / Case Studies Grid ]
                  │
                  ▼
[ Simple, Transparent Services & Pricing ]
                  │
                  ▼
[ Quick, Single-Field Contact Form ]
```

When someone scrolls down your page, they see your work, understand your value, and can immediately fill out your contact form. This structure reduces friction and increases your conversion rate.
